The PANDA Project

Parenting, Adversity, Neurodevelopment, and Developmental Assessment

The Problem

Children from low socioeconomic status (SES) backgrounds face significant academic disadvantages before they ever enter a classroom. Research shows these children are exposed to language delays that begin as early as 18 months, resulting in vocabulary gaps of up to 30 million words by age 3. Without intervention, these early disparities compound into lifelong educational and economic challenges.

Our Research Question

How can we leverage neuroscience research to create accessible, engaging interventions that empower parents and optimize early brain development in underserved children aged 3-5?

The PANDA Intervention

Parent Neuroscience Education

Evidence-based curriculum teaching parents about brain development, neuroplasticity, and actionable strategies to support their child's cognitive growth through everyday interactions.

Child "Learning Lab" Interface

An interactive digital platform designed for ages 3-5 featuring engaging literacy games, vocabulary building activities, and progress tracking that makes learning fun and accessible.

Developmental Assessment

Validated survey instruments to measure parental engagement, child language development, and intervention effectiveness through longitudinal tracking.

Making Neuroscience Accessible

The PANDA app brings evidence-based early learning directly to families, removing barriers to quality developmental support. Designed specifically for underserved communities, the app works on low-cost devices and requires minimal data.

  • Interactive Literacy Games Engaging activities that build vocabulary, phonemic awareness, and reading readiness
  • Parent Education Hub Bite-sized neuroscience lessons with practical tips for everyday learning moments
  • Progress Tracking Visual dashboards showing child development milestones and engagement metrics
  • Accessibility First Designed for low-bandwidth environments and low-cost devices
  • Research-Backed Content All activities grounded in developmental psychology and neuroscience literature

Maximizing Early Brain Development in Underserved Children

Rahiem Jerome Brooks

Key Findings

  • Low-SES children show language delays starting at 18 months, with vocabulary gaps affecting long-term academic outcomes
  • Early intervention during critical periods (ages 3-5) can leverage neuroplasticity to close developmental gaps
  • Parent engagement and neuroscience education significantly improve child literacy outcomes
  • Technology-based interventions can increase accessibility for underserved families
